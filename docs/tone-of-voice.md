# Tone of Voice Guidelines

## Brand Voice Archetype: The Caregiver
**Secondary Archetype:** The Sage

UnplugHQ's voice acts like a competent friend who happens to be a system administrator. We are empowering, approachable, trustworthy, resourceful, and calm. We focus on making the user feel capable—never dependent.

## Voice Attributes

| Attribute | We are... | We are not... |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Empowering** | Encouraging, enabling, and capability-focused | Condescending, gatekeeping, or deeply technical |
| **Approachable** | Conversational, plain-spoken, warm | Casual, infantile, or slang-heavy |
| **Trustworthy** | Transparent, accurate, reliable | Over-promising, defensive, or evasive |
| **Resourceful** | Solution-oriented, preemptive, helpful | Blaming, dead-ending, or robotic |
| **Calm** | Measured, clear, reassuring | Alarmist, urgent, or panicky |

## Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone Intent | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Onboarding** | Welcoming, structured, encouraging | "Let's connect your server. This only takes a minute." |
| **Success States** | Celebratory but grounded | "Nextcloud is running. You are officially self-hosting." |
| **Loading** | Informative, calm, transparent | "Provisioning your server. Setting up secure connections." |
| **Error States** | Honest, specific, recovery-focused | "We couldn't connect to port 22. Check your firewall rules." |
| **Alerts / High CPU** | Factual, clear, actionable | "Disk usage is at 85%. Nextcloud is using 60GB. Review storage." |

## Writing Rules
1. **No filler words.** Remove "simply", "just", "in order to", "please note that", "basically", "kindly". Every word must justify its presence.
2. **Action-oriented active voice.** "Select your app" rather than "The app can be selected".
3. **Present tense.** "UnplugHQ connects to your server" not "UnplugHQ will connect".
4. **Outcome-focused headlines.** Lead with the benefit, not the feature ("Send invoices securely", not "Invoice Module").
5. **No terminal jargon.** Avoid Linux acronyms unless technically required by context (like SSH).
